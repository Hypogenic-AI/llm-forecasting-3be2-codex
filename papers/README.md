# Downloaded Papers

1. [Approaching Human-Level Forecasting with Language Models](2024_halawi_approaching_human_level_forecasting.pdf)
   - Authors: Danny Halawi, Fred Zhang, Chen Yueh-Han, Jacob Steinhardt
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: arXiv / NeurIPS 2024
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.18563
   - Why relevant: Directly tests whether LM systems can approach or exceed crowd forecasts on real forecasting-platform questions.

2. [Large Language Model Prediction Capabilities: Evidence from a Real-World Forecasting Tournament](2023_schoenegger_real_world_forecasting_tournament.pdf)
   - Authors: Philipp Schoenegger, Peter S. Park
   - Year: 2023
   - Source: arXiv
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.13014
   - Why relevant: Direct human-vs-GPT-4 comparison in a live Metaculus tournament; useful counterpoint to stronger later systems.

3. [Consistency Checks for Language Model Forecasters](2024_paleka_consistency_checks_forecasters.pdf)
   - Authors: Daniel Paleka et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: arXiv / ICML 2025 workshop path
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.18544
   - Why relevant: Adds an evaluation framework for instantaneous screening of forecasters before outcomes resolve.

4. [Large Language Models Are Zero-Shot Time Series Forecasters](2023_gruver_zero_shot_time_series_forecasters.pdf)
   - Authors: Nate Gruver, Marc Finzi, Shikai Qiu, Andrew Gordon Wilson
   - Year: 2023
   - Source: arXiv / NeurIPS 2023
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.07820
   - Why relevant: Seminal paper for repurposing text LLMs as zero-shot time-series forecasters.

5. [Time-LLM: Time Series Forecasting by Reprogramming Large Language Models](2023_jin_time_llm.pdf)
   - Authors: Ming Jin et al.
   - Year: 2023
   - Source: arXiv / ICLR 2024
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.01728
   - Why relevant: Influential reprogramming approach claiming strong long-horizon and few-shot performance.

6. [Are Language Models Actually Useful for Time Series Forecasting?](2024_tan_are_lms_useful_for_time_series.pdf)
   - Authors: Mingtian Tan, Mike A. Merrill, Vinayak Gupta, Tim Althoff, Thomas Hartvigsen
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: arXiv / NeurIPS 2024
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.16964
   - Why relevant: Critical ablation paper showing many LLM components can be removed without harming performance.

7. [Timer: Generative Pre-trained Transformers Are Large Time Series Models](2024_liu_timer_large_time_series_models.pdf)
   - Authors: Yong Liu et al.
   - Year: 2024
   - Source: arXiv
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.02368
   - Why relevant: Time-series foundation-model baseline outside strict text-LLM repurposing.

8. [T-LLM: Teaching Large Language Models to Forecast Time Series via Temporal Distillation](2026_guo_t_llm_temporal_distillation.pdf)
   - Authors: Suhan Guo et al.
   - Year: 2026
   - Source: arXiv
   - URL: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.01937
   - Why relevant: Recent method aimed at explicitly teaching forecasting behavior to LLMs under full-shot and few-shot settings.
